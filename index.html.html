Anna & kids — The Narrows
  • Start by 6–7 AM — shuttle opens at 6 AM in June. Early start means cooler temps, fewer people, and better light in the canyon.
  • Take shuttle to Stop 9 (Temple of Sinawava). First mile is paved riverside walk, then you wade into the Virgin River.
  • Water shoes + neoprene socks + trekking poles are essential. The river is cold and the riverbed is very slippery.
  • Plan 3–5 hours and turn back before anyone is exhausted — the best scenery is in the first 2 miles.
Jason — Shuttle sightseeing
  • Free Zion Canyon Shuttle hits all major viewpoints — private cars not allowed on the main road in summer.
  • Best stops: Court of the Patriarchs (Stop 4), Zion Lodge porch (Stop 5) for a cold drink, Weeping Rock (Stop 7) — short easy trail with hanging gardens.
  • Canyon Overlook Trail near the park entrance tunnel — 1 mile, panoramic views, very manageable.
🌡️ Zion hits 95°F+ by noon in June. Plan all hiking done by midday. Afternoon = VRBO, shade, and cold drinks. Oscar's Cafe or Zion Brewing Co. for dinner.

Scenic detour en route
  • Horseshoe Bend, Page AZ — only 15 min off US-89. Short 1.5-mile round-trip walk to an iconic Colorado River horseshoe overlook. Great for kids. Don't skip it.
  • Enter Grand Canyon at the South Entrance. Use your America the Beautiful Pass.
Sightseeing & sunset
  • Mather Point — first major viewpoint after the entrance. Classic and breathtaking.
  • Bright Angel Trail — walk 1–1.5 miles down for the real canyon feeling. No full descent in June heat.
  • Hopi Point sunset (Hermit Road Shuttle Stop 7) — #1 rated sunset spot on the South Rim. Arrive 45 min early. Sunset ~8:00–8:15 PM MST.
  • Dinner: Bright Angel Restaurant (casual, no reservation) or El Tovar if reserved in advance.
🌡️ The Grand Canyon South Rim sits at 7,000 ft — mornings are cool (~47°F), so pack a layer for early walks. It warms fast once the sun is up.

Delicate Arch hike
  • No timed entry reservation required in 2026 — just show up! Aim to be at the gate before 8 AM to beat the crowds and heat.
  • Start early — the exposed slickrock becomes brutally hot by mid-morning. 3 miles round trip, 480 ft gain, zero shade.
  • 2+ liters of water per person, sunscreen, hats — non-negotiable in June heat.
  • Quick additions: Balanced Rock (roadside, 5 min), the Windows Section (easy ½ mi, multiple arches).
Rest of the day in Moab
  • After the morning hike, relax in Moab — pool, cold drinks, browse Main Street shops.
  • Canyonlands National Park viewpoints (Island in the Sky) are a great afternoon scenic drive if energy allows.
  • Early to bed — driving to Flaming Gorge tomorrow.
🌅 Early start pays off: Arrive before 8 AM to beat the crowds — no reservation needed in 2026, so everyone will be showing up. Early also means cooler temps on the trail.

Fishing day
  • Get on the water at sunrise (~5:45 AM) — first couple hours are the most productive for lake trout and bass. Dress in warm layers; it'll be 42–45°F on the water at dawn.
  • Flaming Gorge is world-class — lake trout in deeper sections, kokanee salmon and smallmouth bass in shallower bays. The charter captain will put you on fish.
  • Bait, tackle, rods, and all fishing gear provided by the charter. Just bring snacks, drinks, sunscreen, and a good attitude!
  • Fish through the morning and early afternoon, then pack up for the drive.
Evening drive to Rock Springs or Green River
  • Rock Springs, WY (recommended) — ~45 miles, ~45 min. Better hotel options (Hampton Inn, Holiday Inn Express, Homewood Suites) and more restaurants.
  • Green River, WY — ~35 miles, ~35 min. Quieter and closer, fewer amenities.
